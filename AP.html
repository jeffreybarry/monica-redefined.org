After 10 years of "self-imposed reticence," Monica Lewinsky reintroduced herself to the world in May of 2014 with these powerful words. Her Vanity Fair article Shame and Survival gave her a chance to tell the world her side of the story.

Monica on Twitter: October 2014

Only 5 months after her Vanity Fair article ran, Monica amplified her voice even more. By joining Twitter, she was diving headfirst into the online community that once made her life a living nightmare. Her first tweet on October 20, 2014 read:

#HereWeGo

— Monica Lewinsky (@MonicaLewinsky) October 20, 2014

The tedTalk: March 2015

After 10 months of gradual reintroduction into the public eye, Monica gave the tedTalk that would truly kickstart her new chapter. "The Price of Shame" provided a platform allowing her to publicly discuss the media firestorm of 1998. She referred to herself as "Patient Zero" of losing reputation on a public scale as a result of the 24 hour media cycle.

Following her talk, Monica tweeted this:

Back in Vanity Fair: March 2018

As the #metoo movement took off, women across the world began to share their stories of sexual harassment. For years, Monica had firmly maintained that her relationship with Bill Clinton was consensual. She had echoed that sentiment in her first essay for Vanity Fair. This time was different. She penned Emerging from "The House of Gaslight" in the Age of #metoo as the first admission that her relationship with the President of the United States as a 22-year-old was laced with problems.

John Oliver: March 2019

Monica took a big step in early 2019 as she sat for an interview with John Oliver, a comedian who had made jokes at her expense during the events of 1998. Oliver apologized for how he spoke about her at the time and used her presence as a way to draw attention to the culture of public shaming present in today's online world.

These Days, Monica is:

an anti-bullying activist,

She is a strategic advisor for Bystander Revolution and recently took part in the launch of an interactive anti-cyber bullying campaign entitled The Epidemic. The campaign utilizes text messages to illustrate to viewers the far-reaching consequences of cyberbullying.

a producer,

It was announced in August of 2019 that Lewinsky would join American Crime Story as a producer for their series on the Clinton impeachment. The series will premiere on September 27, 2020.

Andy Warhol once said that "in the future, everyone will be world-famous for 15 minutes." Monica Lewinsky has been world famous for far longer than that. In fact, if we're counting, it has been exactly minutes since Monica Lewinsky's name became a household one.

But, Monica has not allowed 1998 to be the last chapter in her story. She's used the fame and notoriety and has turned it into something good. In reintroducing herself to society, she's begun to take back her name.

"Monica Lewinsky" has been redefined.
