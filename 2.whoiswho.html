Starr’s of the Scandal

Monica Lewinsky

Monica Lewinsky was the young woman whose life was turned upside down after she had an affair with then-President Bill Clinton. She has since become an anti-bullying activist, motivational speaker, and has dabbled in a few entrepreneurial endeavors. Still, Monica spent years struggling to find not only a job but also a self-identity beyond the scandal.

Juanita Broaddrick

Juanita Broaddrick alleged in 1999 that President Bill Clinton raped her in April 1978 while he was the attorney general of Arkansas.

Kathleen Willey

Kathleen Willey was a former White House volunteer who accused Bill Clinton of sexual assault during a March 1998 interview with 60 Minutes. The alleged assault occurred in November 1993. She testified in the Paula Jones sexual harassment lawsuit.

Paula Jones

Paula Jones was an Arkansas state employee who sued president Bill Clinton for sexual harassment. Her lawsuit gave Independent Counsel Kenneth Starr the justification he needed to broaden an existing investigation. During a deposition in the lawsuit, Clinton denied under oath that he had had sexual relations with Monica. His deposition later provided the foundation for an article of impeachment. The Jones lawsuit set a landmark legal precedent that a sitting U.S. president is not exempt from civil litigation for acts committed outside of public office.

Gennifer Flowers

A former TV reporter and Arkansas state employee, she testified in the Paula Jones lawsuit that she had a 12-year affair.

Kenneth Starr

Kenneth Starr was an independent counsel, solicitor general and a federal appelate court judge. Starr was appointed to investigate the failed Whitewater real-estate deal and other controversies involving President Clinton. Congress decided to pubicly release The Starr Report launching the impeachment hearings.

Vernon Jordan

was a member of Clinton’s 1992-93 transition team. He also was a longtime advisor and friend of Clinton. In a public statement, Jordan said he had two interactions with Monica: assisting her in a job search by referring her for interviews at American Express and introducing her to Washington Lawyer Francis Carter.

Newt Gingrich

Newt Gingrich was the Republican Speaker of the House at the time of the Clinton-Lewinsky scandal. He resigned after the 1998 midterm election when Democrats gained ground in the House.

Current Senator from South Carolina, Lindsey Graham, was a key prosecutor for the House of Representatives’ impeachment case against Clinton in 1999.

A current Supreme Court Justice, Brett Kavanaugh co-authored the Starr Report released in 1998. He vehemently argued for the President’s impeachment, urging Starr to ask Clinton sexually graphic questions during the trial. Kavanaugh described Clinton as a liar who obstructed justice and a disgrace to the American people.

Linda Tripp

Linda Tripp was a co-worker of Monica's at the Pentagon. Tripp surreptitiously recorded phone conversations where Monica confided in her about the affair. Tripp later turned these tapes over to Starr. She also told Paula Jones's legal team about Monica's relationship with Clinton before the President gave his deposition in the lawsuit.
