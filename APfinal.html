The TED Talk: March 2015

Monica gave the TED Talk on March 19, 2015 that kickstarted her new chapter. "The Price of Shame" provided a platform that allowed her to publicly discuss the media firestorm of 1998. She referred to herself as "Patient Zero," who lost her reputation becase of the 24-hour media cycle.

Following her talk, Monica tweeted:

Back in Vanity Fair: March 2018

As the #metoo movement took off, women across the world began to share their stories of sexual harassment. For years, Monica had firmly maintained that her relationship with Bill Clinton was consensual. She had echoed that sentiment in her first essay for Vanity Fair. This time was different. She penned Emerging from "The House of Gaslight" in the Age of #metoo. In it, she admitted for the first time that her relationship as a 22-year-old with the President of the United States was problematic.

Monica became a contributing editor to Vanity Fair in 2018.

John Oliver: March 2019

Monica took a big step in early 2019 as she sat for an interview with John Oliver, a comedian who had made jokes at her expense during the events of 1998. Oliver apologized for how he spoke about her and used her presence as a way to draw attention to the culture of public shaming present in today's online world.

an anti-bullying activist,

a producer,

Andy Warhol once said that "in the future, everyone will be world-famous for 15 minutes." Monica has been world-famous for far longer than that. It has been exactly minutes since she became a household name.

But Monica has not allowed 1998 to be the last chapter in her story. She's used the fame and notoriety to take back her name.

Monica Lewinsky has been redefined.
